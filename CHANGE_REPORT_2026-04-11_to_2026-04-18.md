# Change Report

Reporting window: April 11, 2026 to April 18, 2026

Note: there were no commits in this repository on April 11 or April 12. The first changes in this window begin on April 13, 2026.

## Executive Summary

This week was a feature-heavy release cycle focused on assignments, teacher tools, vocabulary expansion, grammar content growth, and fixes to progress tracking and notifications.

Across the reporting window there were:

- 17 commits
- activity on 5 separate days
- 182 files changed
- about 12,210 lines added and 1,206 lines removed

The main themes were:

- a major expansion of assignable activities for teachers and students
- significant growth in grammar and vocabulary content
- stronger reporting and printable admin/teacher workflows
- continued improvements to dictation, live games, and collocations
- a handful of targeted fixes to progress tracking, notifications, and profile counts

## High-Level Changes By Area

### 1. Assignment System Expanded Across More Skills

The biggest product shift this week was the expansion of assignable work across multiple parts of the platform.

- Writing assignments were added, including a new `WritingAssignButton` flow.
- Speaking assignments were added, including a new `SpeakingAssignButton`.
- Vocabulary assignments were added, including a new `VocabAssignButton`.
- Dictation assignments and related notifications were added.
- Reading assignments were added, including a new `ReadingAssignButton`.
- Teachers were also given the ability to reuse previously saved dictation tasks.
- Student request flows for teachers were added alongside reading assignment support.

In practice, this means the class / teacher workflow became much more complete: more activity types can now be assigned, surfaced in class views, and tracked through shared teacher-student tooling.

## 2. Teacher and Admin Workflows Improved

Teacher and admin tooling also saw substantial work.

- Teachers and admins can now print exam reports.
- A large new printable report component was added: `src/components/teacher/TeacherCourseTestPrintableReport.jsx`.
- Teacher dashboards and student-management screens were updated repeatedly during the week.
- Admin dashboard logic was extended several times to support the new assignment and reporting workflows.

This looks like a clear move toward better classroom operations, not just practice content delivery.

## 3. Vocabulary System Grew Significantly

Vocabulary received one of the largest single-day expansions of the week.

- Two new vocabulary topic sets were added: `education` and `tv/cinema`.
- Corresponding dataset files were added:
  - `src/components/vocabulary/data/educationData.js`
  - `src/components/vocabulary/data/tvCinemaData.js`
- A large number of new vocabulary images were added under `public/images/vocab/education/` and `public/images/vocab/tv/`.
- Topic trainer, flashcards, review flows, mistake review, favourites, answer handling, and session utilities were all updated.
- Vocabulary profile count fixes landed afterward to correct counting behavior.

Overall, the vocabulary area gained both more content and more mature learner tooling.

## 4. Grammar Content Expanded

Grammar content also grew materially this week.

- A large batch of mini tests was added on April 15.
- More mini tests for B2 were added on April 17.
- Adverb items were added to the grammar trainer.
- `src/data/hubGrammarActivities.js` saw very large content additions across multiple commits.
- `scripts/grammar-items.json` and the grammar activity runner were updated to support the expanded material.

This suggests a strong push toward richer, level-targeted grammar practice content.

## 5. Dictation and Live Activity Improvements

Several interactive learning tools were expanded or refined.

- New dictation sets for phrasal verbs and prepositions were added, along with many new audio files in `public/audio/dictation/`.
- The dictation trainer and assignment flows were adjusted multiple times during the week.
- Additional fixes were made to the dictation game on April 16.
- The Aptis grammar live game received changes and new host round audio assets.
- Collocation Dash underwent a major rework described in commit history as "radical changes".
- Spanglish Fixit also received supporting changes in the same update that introduced vocabulary assignments.

This part of the week looks like a mix of new content, improved game feel, and workflow stabilization.

## 6. Tracking, Notifications, and Profile Fixes

A smaller but important thread through the week was correctness and UX polish.

- Reading progress tracking was fixed.
- A notifications bug in `src/App.jsx` was fixed.
- Vocabulary profile count fixes were added.
- Firebase and rules updates were made in support of new assignment/reporting behavior.
- `firestore.rules` changed more than once, indicating backend permission/support work alongside frontend features.

These changes matter because they reduce friction around the larger feature rollout.
